The Boy from Class 9

The First Sight

It started in the hallways of my college. I was a student there, finding my way, and he was still in school. I remember the first time my eyes landed on him. He wasn't just handsome; he was beautiful in a way that made time stop. I felt a pull, a sudden rush of love, but my heart was heavier than my courage.

Every day, I went to college just for that one glimpse. He would come, unaware that someone was watching him with so much adoration. I wanted to speak, to say "I love you," or even just "Hello," but my confidence always failed me. I remained a shadow in his bright world.

Drifting Apart

Time didn't wait for my courage. I was promoted to Class 12, and he moved from 8th to 9th grade. I used to dream up scenarios where I would finally talk to him, but in reality, he didn't even know I existed.

My last vivid memory of seeing him casually was near the bathroom area. He looked radiant. Seeing him made my heart race with happiness. But now... life has moved on. I have graduated from college, and he is currently in Class 10. The distance between us has grown from hallway to miles.

The Prayer Together

We prayed Namaz side by side. When we finished, I stood for 2 more rakats, but he started to leave. Suddenly, I said, "Wooo... don't you want to pray 2 more?" He stopped, smiled, and joined me. That silence shared in prayer was everything.

The First Sound

At the bathroom area performing Wudu, I desperately wanted to hear his voice. I asked: "Is Anointing allowed on socks?" He flashed that beautiful smile and said, "I really don't know." It is a melody I still remember.

The Touch

At the canteen, he stood so close he kept his right hand on my shoulder. I froze. I playfully took one toffee from his pile. He looked at me, smiled, and walked away. The sweetness of that moment lasted forever.

I Miss You

You probably don't remember the guy you prayed next to, or the guy who took your toffee. But here I am, graduating and moving forward, yet a part of me is still standing in that college hallway, waiting for you.
